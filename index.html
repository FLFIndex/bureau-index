Index of Bureaucracy

The Index of Bureaucracy shows the time that bureaucratic procedures demand from Small & Medium companies, both to open and to remain formal and legally in operation. The goal of the Index of Bureaucracy is to serve as a basis for advancing a roadmap for redesigning public policies aimed at developing a dynamic and productive economy, making its public sector an enabler for prosperity.

What counts as a bureaucratic burden

For the purposes of this study, a bureaucratic burden is recognized as that which is required by a public authority and that would not be necessary to comply with the demands of the market, or that would be much less and/or more flexible, if it were not for the regulation imposed.

Methodology guidelines

  • Attention to the differences in the productive structures of each country
  • Focus on medium-sized enterprises, following national definitions for this purpose
  • Two groups of bureaucratic procedures: those required to open a business until it is start-up, and those required to keep the company running legal and formally
  • Information gathered with professionals and validated with businesspeople
  • Unit of analysis is the country/territory; unit of measurement is time — hours for opening procedures, annual hours for running procedures
  • Metrics report the time that the mandatory bureaucratic burden demands from businesses

Opening a Business

5 procedure groups

  • I&R — Identification and Registration
  • NAT — Registration with national public entities
  • MUN — Municipal registration and permits
  • SERV — Basic Services Request
  • ACTV — Specific to the productive activity

Running a Business

11 sub-groups in 3 groups

  • Administration of Employment — Payroll, taxes & contributions; Hiring/Firing; Work reports
  • Administration of Operations — Taxes; Waste Management; Vehicles; Health & Safety
  • Others — Legal Updates; Licenses & Certifications; Supervision & Inspections; Activity-specific

Closing a Business

3 procedure groups

  • Dissolution (DIS) — the legal act formalizing the cessation of activities
  • Liquidation (LIQ) — managing assets and liabilities to definitively conclude operations
  • Registration & Notification (R&N) — legal and administrative termination before competent authorities

Small & medium enterprise compliance burden

Index of Bureaucracy

Hours a formal SME must spend on mandatory procedures to open, run, and close — 27 countries/territories, 2021–2026.

Opening a business now costs an average of 1,638 hours of procedure time — about 9.5 business months — and running one legally absorbs a further 1,178 hours a year, equal to more than half of a full-time employee's schedule. Closing down formally is the harshest stage: 3,654 hours on average, driven mostly by liquidation. Across the sample, the private sector spends roughly $33 billion a year on this administrative overhead alone, and the forgone productivity is estimated at $140 billion in GDP — about 16.5% of the GDP of the countries studied.
